speaking test

I just finished my first IELTS speaking test the other day. I haven’t taken the speaking test before, so I was very nervous before the test. And the first time the oral test is more British, I am not familiar with their communication style, which makes me more nervous.
Before the exam, I practiced every oral topic with my teacher repeatedly. At the beginning, I talked very sluggishly and I didn’t have any ideas yet. So I wrote down the topic for which I had no idea. Although some content is still not enough, with the help of the teacher, I added a lot of new content. Finally, after repeated practice, my spoken English became much fluent.
Although I practiced many times before the exam, I felt that I was fully prepared, and I was still very nervous on the day of the exam. My parents saw my nervousness at the time, and they repeatedly encouraged me. With their encouragement, my tension gradually weakened. When I arrived at the exam waiting hall, I read the spoken topic several times and spoke in a low voice. Approaching the exam, I repeatedly calmed my mood.
During the exam, I felt that I was more fluent and confident than usual practice. Through this incident, I understand that when facing something unfamiliar to me, I must be fully prepared before this. Only when you are fully prepared can you be more confident.
